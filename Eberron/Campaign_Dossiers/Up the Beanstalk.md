Introduction
A priceless artifact has been stolen under
unusual circumstances. Its current location?
Sharn, City of Towers, being auctioned off by
person(s) unknown. The current asking price?
Evidence of a conspiracy that could destroy the
fragile peace of Khorvaire if it is successfully
retrieved from the Mournland.

The Cost of Doing Business

    “The tiefling murmurs something too quietly to
    hear and wiggles her fingers at you without so
    much as a glance in your direction. The magic
    burrows into your brain and tongue.
        “’What is said here, is said only here,’ the
    cloud giant whispers.”

After the PCs have chosen their artifact and
written an acceptable bid on one of the cards,
Noctilucent misty steps through the mirror and
opens negotiations by giving them a simple
choice: submit to bind or die. If the party refuses
to let Jak cast the spell, combat erupts (stats for
NPCs begin on page 14).
     If they agree to the giant’s terms, Jak casts
bind (her choice of topic is always “Noctilucent’s
business”) and Noctilucent gives the PCs the
following information:

•     She is in possession of sworn testimony
      stating that the Mourning occurred when an
      artifact created by the Canniths was used by
      the Boromar clan.
•     According to the informant, proof of this can
      be found in the ruins of their Houses in
      Making, deep within the Glass Plateau.
•     The missing d’Canniths are burning out their
      dragonmarks in her workshop trying to
      create items that offer protection against the
      effects of the Mournland.
•     The informant’s claims must be checked; this
      is her price for [the artifact]. If proof exists,
      the group must bring it back from Cyre. No
      proof, no [the artifact].

    Noctilucent gives each PC a black pebble and
instructions to take the pebbles to a warehouse
in Precarious (its sign is a big blue cloud).
    When the players are ready to depart the
auction, the shifter brings them back where they
started. If the PCs haven’t been aggressive to her
and can pay, Scratch now mentions that she can
send them to both Precarious and Making.

Precarious (Cliffside)
Noctilucent’s warehouse gnomes will exchange
the pebbles for mournsuits created by the
abducted artificers. See New Magic on page 16
for details.
    The gnomes are skeptical of the suits
because none of the “test subjects” have come
back. They have also had bind cast on them and
cannot comment further on the cloud giant’s
business.
    A large crate arrives while the PCs are there.
It contains more mournsuits and a note claiming
this batch is sturdier than the last. These are
made of a stretchy cotton, while the original
suits are flimsy organza. Players may wear either
version; the suits function identically.

    include houses of worship, banking and
    commerce guaranteed to contain priceless goods.

            Endless Possibilities
    The events in Up the Beanstalk have the potential to
    spawn many additional adventures, including:

    •   If the PCs do not give the proof to Noctilucent,
        she will try to use her gloves of grasping on it.
    •   If the PCs encountered the Queen’s bodyguards,
        they may discover she is still alive.
    •   If the PCs spurn their offer to become House
        Agents, the Canniths may send assassins after
        them.
    •   If the PCs make it through the adventure with
        intact mournsuits, they may wish to explore Cyre
        further.
    •   If the PCs become members of Blue Sky, they
        may find themselves being stalked by Boromar
        assassins.
    •   If the PCs gave the evidence to the Boromar
        clan, they may be abducted and interrogated by
        Daask Inquisitors.
    •   If the PCs gave the evidence to Prince Oargev, he
        may hire them to deliver retribution.
    •   If the PCs become members of Blue Sky, they
        may have reason to travel to Xen’drik..
    •   If the PCs gave the evidence to the newspapers,
        they may have reignited the Last War.

    Future adventures. Unless betrayed by the group,
    Noctilucent continues to be a power player in Sharn.
    Some salient points for designing future adventures
    around the giant and the Beanstalk include:

    •   She always has rumors that need to be
        investigated.
    •   Anyone can try to book an appointment at the
        spa.
    •   The fire giant does not live there of his own
        accord.
    •   The Beanstalk is a formidable assault weapon
        that can be deployed anywhere.
    •   Noctilucent pays loyal employees very well.

        If the group betrayed the cloud giant:

    •   She can periodically send assassins after them.
    •   She can use her gloves of grasping if she knows
        where they are or will be.
    •   Even if forced to flee Sharn, Noctilucent has a
        long reach.
    •   The Beanstalk is a formidable assault weapon
        that can be deployed anywhere!

        Members of Blue Sky may be surprised to
    discover just how far Noctilucent’s reach extends
    outside of Sharn. PCs who bear the permanent
    tattoo could receive all manner of special benefits
    ranging from better prices to extra information as
    they make their way across Khorvaire.
