A Journey Like No Other
                y name is Captain Nix Macbradach and I




M
                 am the greatest sailor and pirate you’ve
                 ever heard of! Hero to millions, hated
                 and loved across the multiverse!
                 Though, truthfully, I’ve been a bit more
                 reclusive of late; the life of an adventurer
                 is glamourous and thrilling, but I’ve
been crowded by adoring fans and zealous suitors for so
long that I’ve lost the thing that I most enjoyed. I crave
the simple freedom of the open ocean, the salty sweet
air and a strong breeze at my back. Mine is a soul meant
to wander, and I’ve been left to stagnate for too long!
   Before I retire and return to my life at sea, my agents
have asked that I produce one final volume for them. A
tome detailing the wonderous worlds and amazing
creatures I’ve seen whilst aboard my great airship, the
Calamity. Her thunderous wake carried my crew and I
across shimmering emerald seas, over vast broiling
dunes of golden sand, deep inside the towering black
forests of the darkest feywild, and to planes so remote
and untouched that the meer mention of the material
world had yet to reach the ears of their inhabitants. The
journey was treacherous and many of my brave
crewmen never returned. I hope to make their sacrifice
worth it by chronicling our adventures in this tome for
all to read and enjoy!
   In this book I’ve included sketches and descriptions
of the strange creatures and fantastic civilizations we
encountered, as well as maps of the lands they
inhabited.

  have squat bodies and large bat-like ears. Their large
  round eyes help them see in the dark of their
  underground tunnels and forage on the surface at night,
  avoiding the desert heat. Their flat, slitted nostrils can
  be sealed shut to keep sand and other debris out of their
  noses. Their rough skin tends to be earthen toned,
  appearing in splotchy patterns of grey, red, brown, and
  tan. Their course reddish hair grows in a short mane
  that sprouts from the tops of their heads and continues
  down to the base of their necks.
     Bhukas live in large subterranean communities,
  tending to mushroom farms for most of their dietary
  needs. They craft intricate clay pottery for carrying
  water from underground springs. Bhukas are highly
  communal, valuing the good of the community over
  individual needs. They have a robust medical tradition
  and take excellent care of their wounded and sick.
     Bhukas are pacifistic, preferring to burrow out of
  trouble than face it head on. However, when their home
  or family are threatened, bhuka can become fierce
  defenders.

Life at sea is exceedingly dangerous, even the toughest
of sailors must be cautious. Barbarians that follow the
path of the leviathan take inspiration from the mighty
tentacled beasts of the sea, drawing their foes in to fight
them on their own terms. Many barbarians that follow
the path of the leviathan eventualy choose to test their
skills against the crushing might of the very monsters
from which the draw their power.
Crushing Grip
3rd-level Path of the Leviathan Feature
While you are raging you may spend a bonus action to
deal bludgeoning damage equal to your Strength
modifier to a creature you have grappled. Additionally,
you may attempt to grapple creatures up to two sizes
larger than you, or up to two creatures of your size or
smaller.
Undertow
6th-level Path of the Leviathan Feature
You can call upon the power of the tides to haul your
prey toward your waiting arms. When you hit a creature
with a net or other thrown weapon, you may attempt to
pull the target toward you. The target must make a
Strength save against a DC equal to 8 + your Strength
modifier + your proficiency bonus. If it fails, the target is
pulled a number of feet equal to the difference toward
you (minimum 5 feet). Additionally, nets you throw may
restrain a creature up to huge size.
Kraken’s Fortitude
10th-level Path of the Leviathan Feature
You manifest the fortitude of the mighty kraken. You
may grapple creatures of any size. Additionally, you gain
a swim speed equal to your walking speed and can
breath underwater.
To the Depths
14th-level Path of the Leviathan Feature

yourself, bright light in the zone is reduced to dim light,

activate this feature, you can summon a number of
ethereal tentacles equal to your Wisdom modifier in
unoccupied spaces within the zone. Whenever you
would make an attack, you may instead make the attack
with one of the tentacles. The tentacles use your
Strength modifier for their attack rolls and deal 1d8 +
your Strength modifier bludgeoning damage.

     Corrosion
       Corrosion is a condition applied to objects and
       constructs.
       When an object reaches 3 levels of corrosion, it
       breaks.
       When a Magic Item reaches 3 levels of corrosion, it’s
       magic is suppressed until it can be fully repaired.
       A construct suffers a -1 penalty to its constitution
       score for each level of corrosion; when it reaches 0
       constitution, it crumbles to dust.
       Certain spells and effects can also apply a level of
       corrosion.
       A level of corrosion can be removed by casting
       mending, or by taking 1 hour and making an
       appropriate tool check.
     Dying
       Gained when a player character reaches 0hp.
       The creature is prone.
       The creature may take only a single action, bonus
       action, or move action on their turn. If the creature
       takes takes no actions, they may make a reaction
       before the start of their next turn.
       The creature must make a Death Saving Throw at
       the start of every turn. On a roll of 10 or higher, the
       creature regains 1 hitpoint and immediately stands
       from prone. On a roll of 9 or lower, the creature
       suffers a level of exhaustion.
     Embarassed
       An embarassed creature has disadvantage on
       Deception, Intimidation, and Persuasion checks, as
       well as Charisma saving throws.
       Any roll the creature makes with a result of 2 or less
       is considered a fumble.
     Exhaustion (alt)
       A creature suffers a cummulative -1 penalty to all
       rolls equal to their level of exhaustion.
       When a creature reaches 10 levels of Exhaustion,
       they die.
     Marked
       Applied when a creature hits another creature with a
       melee attack.
       A mark lasts until the start of the attackers next turn.
       While a creature is marked, the creature that marked
       it may make a single opportunity attack against it
       without using a reaction.
