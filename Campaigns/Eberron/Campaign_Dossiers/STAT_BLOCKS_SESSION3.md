# VAULT OF WHISPERS — SESSION 3 STAT BLOCKS
*Korranberg: Chief Registrar Vorna d'Sivis & The Korranberg Overseer*
*Created: 2026-04-27 | For 8-player party at Level 3*

## CHIEF REGISTRAR VORNA D'SIVIS
*Small humanoid (gnome), lawful neutral*

**Armor Class** 13 (bureaucrat's layered robes, reinforced with Sivis wards)
**Hit Points** 52 (8d6 + 24)
**Speed** 25 ft.

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 8 (-1) | 14 (+2) | 16 (+3) | 18 (+4) | 16 (+3) | 15 (+2) |

**Saving Throws** Int +6, Wis +5
**Skills** History +6, Insight +7, Perception +5, Persuasion +4, Investigation +6
**Damage Resistances** Psychic
**Condition Immunities** Frightened, Charmed
**Senses** Darkvision 60 ft., passive Perception 15
**Languages** Common, Gnomish, Deep Speech (reads only), Draconic
**Challenge** 3 (700 XP)

---

### TRAITS

**Sivis Goggle Apparatus.**
Vorna's multi-lensed goggles grant her advantage on Perception and Investigation checks. However, when she attempts to perceive a creature carrying a Gift of the Glimpse or Black Slumber, her goggles malfunction — clicking and spinning wildly. She is unable to read that creature's records or identity, and is Stunned until the end of her next turn the first time this happens per encounter.

**Living Ledger.**
Vorna has memorized the manifest of every person who has entered Korranberg in the past year. If a creature lies to her about their identity, she knows it automatically — no check required — and silently adds a Red Flag to their file (+2 DC to all future social checks in Korranberg).

**Iridescent Ink Curse.**
Vorna's fingers are permanently stained with shimmering Sivis ink. Any creature she marks (as a bonus action, touch) is tracked by the Zilargo Trust. Trust agents arrive in 1d4 hours unless the mark is removed (Remove Curse, DC 13).

**Institutional Authority.**
While inside the Great Library or any Sivis enclave, Vorna can call for 1d4 Sivis Notary Guards (use Veteran stat block, reskinned) as a bonus action. They arrive in 1d3 rounds.

---

### ACTIONS

**Multiattack.** Vorna makes two Ink Quill attacks, or uses Redact once and makes one Ink Quill attack.

**Ink Quill.**
*Melee or Ranged Weapon Attack:* +4 to hit, reach 5 ft. or range 20/60 ft., one target.
*Hit:* 7 (2d4 + 2) piercing damage. The target must succeed on a **DC 13 Constitution saving throw** or have their voice suppressed — unable to speak or cast spells with verbal components until the end of their next turn, as their words are "redacted" from reality.

**Redact (Recharge 5–6).**
Vorna makes a filing notation about one creature she can see within 30 feet. The target must succeed on a **DC 14 Charisma saving throw** or be rendered Invisible to all creatures except Vorna for 1 minute — erased from the social fabric of the room. While invisible this way, the creature cannot interact with any object or NPC (they are "unfiled"). The effect ends early if the creature takes damage.

**Stamp of Condemnation (1/Day).**
Vorna stamps her seal on a surface or creature within 5 feet. If used on a creature, that creature is designated as a "Discarded Document." For the next 24 hours, all Zilargo Trust members, Sivis Notaries, and city guards have advantage on checks to find or identify that creature. The stamp glows faintly violet and cannot be removed without a 5th level or higher Dispel Magic.

---

### REACTIONS

**Clerical Error.**
When Vorna is hit by an attack, she can use her reaction to flip open her ledger and "correct" the attacker's file. The attacker must succeed on a **DC 13 Wisdom saving throw** or have disadvantage on their next attack roll against Vorna, as doubt in their own actions creeps in.

---

### ROLEPLAYING VORNA

*"She doesn't see people. She sees entries. You are either Filed, Misfiled, or Discarded. Right now you are a Clerical Error, and errors are corrected."*

**In negotiation:** Vorna responds to logic, documentation, and procedure — not emotion or intimidation. A DC 16 Persuasion check with a plausible paper trail gets her cooperation. DC 14 Deception with forged documents also works. Pure intimidation automatically fails and triggers Institutional Authority.

**Goggle Malfunction trigger:** The first time Vorna looks directly at Qinmar, Azura, or Karnn (Glimpse Gifted), read aloud:
> *"The gnome's goggle apparatus emits a sharp, mechanical shriek. The lenses spin wildly, clicking against each other like a panicked clock. She stares at you — through you — her expression shifting from cold authority to something deeply, privately unsettled. 'You are... not in the ledger,' she whispers."*

## THE KORRANBERG OVERSEER
*Large construct, lawful neutral*

**Armor Class** 17 (etched brass plating)
**Hit Points** 114 (12d10 + 48)
**Speed** 0 ft., rail-mounted fly 40 ft. (cannot leave ceiling rail system)

| STR | DEX | CON | INT | WIS | CHA |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 18 (+4) | 10 (+0) | 18 (+4) | 17 (+3) | 15 (+2) | 13 (+1) |

**Saving Throws** Con +7, Int +6, Wis +5
**Skills** Perception +8, Investigation +6
**Damage Immunities** Poison, Psychic
**Condition Immunities** Blinded, Charmed, Exhaustion, Frightened, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone
**Senses** Truesight 60 ft., passive Perception 18
**Languages** Common, Gnomish, Deep Speech (output only — it writes, doesn't speak)
**Challenge** 7 (2,900 XP)

---

### TRAITS

**Rail Bound.**
The Overseer moves only along its ceiling rail system. It cannot be knocked prone, pushed, or grappled off its rail. If the rail is destroyed (AC 12, HP 30), the Overseer crashes to the ground, is restrained, and loses its fly speed.

**Overseer's Lens — Truesight.**
The Overseer cannot be deceived by illusions, polymorph, or disguise. It automatically detects all Gifted creatures (Glimpse or Slumber) and writes their Gift designation in its ledger as a free action.

**Void-Static Beam.**
Any creature that starts its turn in the Overseer's violet light beam (30 ft. cone, direction chosen at start of each round) must succeed on a **DC 14 Wisdom saving throw** or be Stunned until the end of their next turn as they relive a Redacted memory.

**Construct Nature.**
The Overseer doesn't require air, food, drink, or sleep.

**Anti-Magic Plating.**
The Overseer has advantage on saving throws against spells. Spell attacks made against it are made at disadvantage.

---

### ACTIONS

**Multiattack.** The Overseer uses its Void-Static Beam (if recharged) and makes two Quill Stab attacks. Or it uses Redaction Ray and makes one Quill Stab attack.

**Quill Stab.**
*Melee Weapon Attack:* +7 to hit, reach 10 ft., one target.
*Hit:* 13 (2d8 + 4) piercing damage. The target must succeed on a **DC 14 Intelligence saving throw** or lose access to one memorized spell slot (chosen randomly) until they complete a short rest — the spell is "redacted" from their mind.

**Redaction Ray.**
*Ranged Spell Attack:* +6 to hit, range 60 ft., one target.
*Hit:* 18 (4d8) force damage. On a hit, the target must succeed on a **DC 15 Charisma saving throw** or be Redacted — rendered Invisible and Phased Out (cannot take actions, move, or be targeted) for 1 round as they are temporarily erased from the battlefield. They reappear at the end of their next turn in the same space.

**Shifting Stacks (Recharge 4–6).**
The Overseer activates the archive's bookshelf mechanism. All bookshelves in the room shift simultaneously. Each creature in the room must succeed on a **DC 13 Dexterity saving throw** or be separated from their group, ending up in a different quadrant of the archive (DM's discretion). Creatures separated this way cannot see or move to their allies without first succeeding on a **DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation)** check (takes their action).

---

### LEGENDARY ACTIONS
*The Overseer can take 2 legendary actions, choosing from the options below. Only one legendary action option can be used at a time and only at the end of another creature's turn. The Overseer regains spent legendary actions at the start of its turn.*

**Reposition.** The Overseer moves up to 20 ft. along its rail.

**Quill Stab (Costs 1 Action).** The Overseer makes one Quill Stab attack.

**Redaction Ray (Costs 2 Actions).** The Overseer uses its Redaction Ray.

---

### REACTIONS

**Automatic Correction.**
When a creature within 30 feet casts a spell, the Overseer can use its reaction to attempt to "correct" the casting. The caster must succeed on a **DC 14 Constitution saving throw** or the spell fails and the slot is expended — the words are "edited out" before they can form.

---

### TACTICAL NOTES FOR DM

**The Overseer's Role:** Stays on its rail, using Void-Static Beam to stun the front line (Karnn, Scrap) while Redaction Ray targets backline casters (Azura, Genesis, Vaelrion). Prioritizes Gifted creatures — it knows what they are and treats them as Priority Documents for "correction."

**Gift Interactions:**
- **Scrap (Archivist's Peace):** Immune to Frightened. The Overseer cannot intimidate him — it will recognize this and escalate to Redaction Ray.
- **Vaelrion (Amnesia of Stars):** The Overseer's Truesight sees through Nondetection — Vaelrion is NOT hidden from it. Great dramatic reveal moment.
- **Karnn (Obsidian Integration):** The rail system (AC 12, HP 30) is an object — Karnn deals double damage to it. Destroying the rail crashes the Overseer and effectively ends the fight.
- **Qinmar (Blindsight 60ft):** Unaffected by Void-Static Beam's visual component. Redacted creatures are still detectable to him as vibrating outlines.
- **Genesis (Lullaby of Xoriat):** His healing grants damage Resistance — keeping allies alive through the Overseer's sustained damage is Genesis's key role in this fight.
- **Berrik (Dead-Eye Dreamer):** Putting the Overseer to sleep won't work (Construct, immune to conditions) — but slowing a summoned guard or Ink-Born with his speed reduction is still valuable.

**The Smart Play:** The party will likely figure out that destroying the rail ends the fight. Let them. That's great D&D — reward creative thinking over straight damage racing.

**Read-Aloud — First Encounter:**
> *"Above you, suspended from a network of darkwood rails bolted into the vaulted ceiling, a six-foot sphere of etched brass rotates slowly. Where eyes should be, a massive amethyst lens pulses with violet light. Its articulated brass rods — each ending in a magnifying lens or silver quill — twitch and focus as it sweeps the room. It makes no sound except the oily, mechanical whisper of its rail system. Then its lens finds you, and the violet light narrows to a beam. Somewhere in its chassis, you hear the scratch of a quill writing."*
