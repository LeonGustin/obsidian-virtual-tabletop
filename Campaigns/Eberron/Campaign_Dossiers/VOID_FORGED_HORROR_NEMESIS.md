# THE VOID-FORGED HORROR — NEMESIS FILE
*Formerly Hrothgrin, Dwarf Aberrant Mind Sorcerer*
*Created: 2026-04-27 | Living Document — Update with each Form*

---

## DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

The Void-Forged Horror is not a monster encounter. It is a **relationship.**

Modeled after Nemesis from Resident Evil 2 — a persistent, evolving threat that the party cannot permanently destroy. Every encounter ends the same way: **the party survives, but the Horror escapes.** It retreats not out of weakness, but because Xal-Kul calls it back to be edited further.

**Core Rules for every Horror encounter:**
1. It is always beatable — but never killable. When reduced to a threshold HP (DM's call per Form), it escapes.
2. It remembers the party. It was Hrothgrin. It knows their faces, fighting styles, and weaknesses.
3. Each new Form is specifically designed around the party's current abilities. What worked last time won't work the same way again.
4. It shows up at the worst possible moment — never when the party is rested and ready.
5. Its appearance is always preceded by a tell — a signature warning the players will eventually learn to fear.

## ORIGIN

**Original Identity:** Hrothgrin, Dwarf Aberrant Mind Sorcerer
- Clan: Narathun (rival of Berrik's Clan Mroranon)
- Weapon: Obsidian Maw — a warhammer containing a shard of Xal-Kul's psionic essence
- Sorcerous Origin: Aberrant Mind — his magic was already touched by Xoriat before the mutation

**The Fall — Session 1, Lightning Rail Carriage 7:**
During combat with a Gibbering Mouther, the Obsidian Maw cracked. The psionic entity — a shard of Xal-Kul — erupted from the hammer and possessed Hrothgrin, triggering a catastrophic physical transformation into the Void-Forged Horror (1st Form).

**What the Party Knows:**
- They watched Hrothgrin transform
- They watched the Horror step through the Xoriat portal at the end of Session 1
- They do not know if it survived
- They likely believe it was consumed by Xoriat or destroyed in the crossing

**What Only the DM Knows:**
- It survived. Xoriat didn't destroy it — it *perfected* it.
- Xal-Kul has been "editing" it since the portal crossing
- It is now a fully conscious agent of the Ink-Stained King
- It remembers everything. Every face. Every spell. Every weakness it observed.
- It is waiting for the right moment to return.

## THE SIGNATURE TELL

Before every Horror encounter, the party receives a warning they will eventually learn to recognize:

> *The air pressure drops. The smell of burnt parchment fills the space. Somewhere nearby, obsidian splinters crack against stone — the sound of a hammer that no longer exists, echoing from a dead man's memory.*

In later encounters, add: violet psionic fire flickering at the edges of their vision. By Form 3+, even seeing this tell should cause experienced players genuine dread.

## 1ST FORM — THE FRESH HORROR
*Already encountered — Session 1, Lightning Rail*
*For reference only*

The raw, violent first mutation. Still partly Hrothgrin — traces of the dwarf's fighting instincts, his rage, his confusion. The most emotionally resonant form because the party can still see their companion inside the monster.

**Key abilities (already seen by party):**
- Psionic Miasma aura (15ft radius)
- Ink-Born Regeneration (10 HP/turn, stopped by radiant)
- Gravity Well (recharge 5-6)
- Psionic Scream (recharge 6)
- Legendary Actions: Shift Reality, Psychic Lash, Obsidian Barrage, Command Ink-Born
- CR 6 | HP 165 | AC 16

**What it retained from Hrothgrin:**
- Dwarf resilience (Con save proficiency)
- Hammer-based fighting instincts (translated into fist/spike attacks)
- Aberrant Mind sorcery — raw, uncontrolled, leaking as environmental psychic damage

**Narrative note:** The party saw it walk into the Xoriat portal. They don't know it walked back out.

## 2ND FORM — THE EDITED HORROR
*Build when party reaches Level 4-5 | Adjust based on party abilities at that time*

Xal-Kul has had time to fully "edit" Hrothgrin. The raw chaos of the 1st Form has been refined into something deliberate and tactical. The Horror no longer fights like a wounded animal — it fights like a predator that has studied its prey.

**Narrative changes:**
- The skin has fully hardened into basalt plating veined with violet psionic fire
- The ribcage maw has closed — replaced by a hollow where Hrothgrin's face used to be, now a smooth obsidian surface with a single glowing eye
- It no longer screams. It is silent. Terrifyingly silent.
- Traces of Hrothgrin are fading — the dwarf's fighting instincts remain, but his personality is gone

**Thematic shift:** From rage to *patience.* It waited. It watched. Now it's here for something specific.

**Design notes for when this Form is built:**
- Review party's current abilities, spells, and Gift mechanics
- Design at least one ability that directly counters the party's most effective tactic from previous encounters
- Its Aberrant Mind sorcery is now controlled — specific spells, not leaking chaos
- Should feel 30-40% harder than 1st Form
- Escape condition: Reduced to ~40 HP — it doesn't flee, it *withdraws.* There is a difference.

**Escape read-aloud (placeholder):**
> *"The single eye dims. The violet fire in its chest collapses inward like a dying star. And then — between one breath and the next — it is simply gone. Not through a portal. Not with a sound. It is edited out of the room, leaving only the smell of burnt parchment and a single obsidian shard on the floor where it stood."*

## 3RD FORM — THE VOID MADE FLESH
*Build when party reaches Level 6-7 | Adjust based on party abilities at that time*

Hrothgrin is gone. There is no dwarf left inside this thing. What remains is a vessel — a perfect instrument of Xal-Kul's will, shaped from the raw material that used to be a person.

**Narrative changes:**
- It no longer resembles a humanoid. The form has shifted — larger, more geometric, wrong in ways that are hard to articulate
- Its movements defy physics in subtle ways — it arrives from directions that don't make sense, occupies space incorrectly
- Looking at it directly requires a Wisdom save — not because of a mechanic, but because the mind resists processing what it sees
- The Aberrant Mind sorcery has fully merged with Xoriat geometry — its spells don't look like spells. They look like reality being edited.

**Thematic shift:** From predator to *inevitability.* It doesn't hunt the party. It simply appears wherever they are, as if the universe has been rewritten to place it there.

**Design notes for when this Form is built:**
- Full rebuild based on party Level 6-7 abilities
- Should feel qualitatively different from Forms 1 and 2 — not just harder, but *stranger*
- Introduce at least one ability the party has never seen before and cannot immediately counter
- The escape this time should feel less like retreat and more like the Horror choosing to leave

## 4TH FORM AND BEYOND
*Endgame — build when relevant*

At this stage the Horror has transcended its origin entirely. It is no longer an edited dwarf. It is a new entity — something Xal-Kul created using Hrothgrin as raw material. Whether it retains any fragment of Hrothgrin's consciousness is a narrative decision for the endgame.

**Potential endgame question:** Can Hrothgrin be saved? Is there enough of him left? This could be a critical moral choice for the party — especially for Berrik (Clan Mroranon), who has a clan-level relationship with Hrothgrin's history.

## DM NOTES — USING THE HORROR EFFECTIVELY

**Timing is everything.** The Horror should never appear when the party is rested, resourced, and ready. It appears after a hard fight, during a critical social scene, when the party is split, or at a moment of emotional vulnerability.

**It is always watching.** Even between encounters, the Horror is present as an offscreen threat. The obsidian hammer head left at the campfire (Session 2) was it. Reminding them. Let the party find similar signs periodically — a shard of basalt in a locked room, the smell of burnt parchment before a long rest, a door that wasn't open before.

**It targets specifically.** Unlike a random monster, the Horror has priorities:
1. Scrap (Vault Map — Xal-Kul's directive)
2. The primary healer (Genesis — disrupt party sustainability)
3. Whoever hurt it most in the previous encounter (personal)

**Berrik's arc.** Nathaniel plays Berrik — dry, dark humor, knows what he's doing. The Horror was Hrothgrin, a rival clan member Berrik was sent to watch. There is history there. Design at least one encounter where the Horror specifically acknowledges Berrik — looks at him differently, targets him last, leaves him alive deliberately. That moment will hit hard.

**The remove curse question.** If the party ever attempts Remove Curse or a similar effect on the Horror, it should react — violently. Xal-Kul feels the attempt through his connection to the Horror and responds. This is a good way to escalate the BBEG's presence without showing him directly.
